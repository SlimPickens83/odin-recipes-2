Meat

Of sundry manners and procurements, having been skewered and set above a mighty flame

  1. Take thou thy fresh kill, skewering lengthwise with iron, and lift it to rest above unrelenting flames.
  2. Order thine manservants to gently rotate the beast, that each side should take the alloted fire thereupon.
  3. A great basin may rest below, that drippings and fats and other waters might collect there.
  4. Adding to this salt, leeks, and carrots, at least, then let the waters agitate and become fragrant.
  5. Once roasted, the meat may be apportioned as each is due, taking the fragrant soup from beneath the beast with each one so as to greatly magnify its flavor.
